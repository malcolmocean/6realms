identifying with what I like → god realm

The highest realm on the wheel, and the most beautiful trap on it. Nothing here hurts. That's the mechanism: with nothing to push against, nothing wakes you. The devas live immensely long, pleasant lives, and the teachings reserve for them a peculiar pity — because the meter is running the whole time, and the fall, when the merit runs out, is the longest one there is.

the shape of the trap

He has achieved a kind of self-hypnosis, a natural state of concentration which blocks out of his mind everything he might find irritating or undesirable.

— Chögyam Trungpa, Cutting Through Spiritual Materialism

So the realm of the gods is not particularly painful, in itself. The pain comes from the eventual disillusionment. You think you have achieved a continually blissful state, spiritual or worldly; you are dwelling on that. But suddenly something shakes you and you realize that what you have achieved is not going to last forever.

— Chögyam Trungpa, The Myth of Freedom

… the god realm concerned with self-infatuation, pride, intoxication of fabricated experience, and indifference to others …

— Martin Lowenthal & Lar Short, Opening the Heart of Compassion

The essence of the god realm is "I am right, and that's just how it is." It's about pride.

— Ken McLeod, Wake Up to Your Life (six realms practice)

The gods enjoy perfect health, comfort, wealth and happiness all their lives. However, they spend their time in diversions and the idea of practising Dharma never occurs to them… Then, having wasted their whole life in distraction, they are suddenly confronted with death.

— Patrul Rinpoche, The Words of My Perfect Teacher

novel

Brave New World — Aldous Huxley

A god realm built by human-realm hubris: the Controllers are certain they've understood society completely ("we've figured it all out"), and what their understanding builds is a heaven of engineered contentment for everyone inside it. Suffering abolished, and meaning along with it.

Christianity without tears — that's what soma is.

— Mustapha Mond, World Controller

And the realm's exit, stated more plainly than any sutra — John the Savage, refusing heaven to its manager's face: "But I don't want comfort. I want God, I want poetry, I want real danger…" — "In fact," said Mustapha Mond, "you're claiming the right to be unhappy." "All right then," said the Savage defiantly, "I'm claiming the right to be unhappy."

film · tv

WALL-E's Axiom — Pixar · The Good Place, season 4 — Michael Schur

Humanity in recliners, every need met, gaze fixed on a screen: the god realm with cup-holders. And The Good Place's final season arrives at the classical diagnosis by sitcom means — even paradise is unbearable without an ending. Its solution is the deva realm's one missing feature: a door.
